Blue Notes

Natural note-taking on tablets and smart phones.

Blue Notes prototype on a 10 inch tablet

A tablet has size and form which are similar to paper notebooks. With an active stylus, it can offer a writing experience which is quite close to paper. With good software, there is the potential to unlock many new possibilities over traditional paper. It's possible to freely erase, reposition, and resize your hand written notes. The page extends automatically when you would have run out of room in a traditional paper notebook. You can even stretch a page to "insert more paper" between existing notes, just like in a word processor.

Blue Notes provides a streamlined note taking experience for use in the class room, meeting room, in a coffee shop, or in your home study. Common actions such as erase, change-pen-color, move-notes, move-page-view require the least hand and finger movements in Blue Notes.

With careful attention to software algorithms, Blue Notes runs quickly and responsively on optimized devices. Full screen mode, pen proximity detection, and smart menus create a natural writing experience on the available screen space. Considering how most people hand-write, the Blue Notes layout allows users to rest their palm on the screen without accidental activations.

An exciting feature of Blue Notes is the ability to create hand drawn animations. A user can circle anything on the screen, then move it along a timeline, creating an animation which lives inside the page. It is an intuitive and lightweight method to illustrate things which change over time, for example. To create more detail, you may also animate frame by frame, much like when creating traditional cartoons.

FAQ

Why is there a need for this app?
Several note taking apps exist for Android devices. Examples are Samsung's S Note, Lecture Notes, Handy Notes, and Papyrus. Unfortunately, they do not offer a good experience when you need to quickly write down ideas while interacting with others. Some apps do not take advantage of the active stylus's capabilities, others put the emphasis on designing for appearance in an offline way, still others on social sharing. These issues result in distractions and wasted effort when taking class notes or meeting notes.
What is the business model?
The basic note taking ability, using input styles chosen as best by the developer, is free. The paid app will provide user customizations which cater to experienced users. Other features in the paid version include cloud data storage for easy backup and sharing, and keyword indexing.

There may be a future direction for Blue Notes in online tutoring. The ability to show your hand-written work to someone else and receive feedback in a structured way is an exciting new area for distance learning.
